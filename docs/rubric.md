# Thesis Rubric & Requirements

Final Thesis Checklist
Wrapping Up Your Thesis!
## Overall Writing
- [ ] Are references correctly placed and used where needed?
- [ ] Are paragraphs consistent?
- [ ] Do you use transitions between paragraphs correctly?
- [ ] Are key terms consistent?
- [ ] Did you read through for grammar edits? (reading aloud helps)
- [ ] Are any technical terms described?
- [ ] Are acronyms introduced appropriately the first time?

## Formatting & References
- [ ] Aim to make references clickable in the text, e.g. by using the package hyperref in LaTeX.
- [ ] Ensure that your references share the same style and information. It's not nice to see the page counting in some and not in others, or the date of access in some but not in others.
- [ ] There shouldn't be Subsections 0, but starting with 1. Sometimes you see Section 3 followed by Subsubsection 3.0.1, you should wrap these subsubsections in a subsection.
- [ ] When mentioning variables' labels in the text, e.g., "t" instead of "time", the variable label should be between $,$.
- [ ] When referencing, be sure you do not repeat things for style, for instance: "A recent study by X et al. (X et al., 2024) indicates that..." should be either something like "A recent study by X et al. (2024) indicates that..." or "A recent study (X et al., 2024) indicates that...".
- [ ] Do NOT include code directly in your thesis - instead, provide a link to a github repository (or similar).
- [ ] Avoid orphan/widow headings (headings that begin at the bottom of a page but the paragraph it pertains to is on the next page).
- [ ] Avoid orphan/widow figure captions (captions that begin at the top of a page but the figure it is referring to is on the previous page).
- [ ] Avoid tables that bleed over to the following page. Move the entire table (with its caption) to the following page.

## Section by Section Considerations
Go through each section and make sure you hit the relevant criteria.
### Abstract - The TL;DR of your Thesis
- [ ] Is the question/purpose stated?
- [ ] Is the experimental approach stated?
- [ ] Are the results briefly summarized?
- [ ] Is the answer/conclusion provided?
- [ ] Has the abstract been condensed as much as possible?
- [ ] Is the context and significance of the work apparent?
- [ ] Does the abstract show that the work was novel?
- [ ] Length target: 1200-300 words ideal.

### Introduction
- [ ] Do you show that the topic is novel?
- [ ] Do you include all relevant background?
- [ ] Is the problem clearly stated?
- [ ] Is the purpose clearly stated?
- [ ] Does your purpose really address the unknown? Does the rest of your thesis really answer this question? Does your thesis only answer part of this? If so, be clear about scope here.
- [ ] Is your approach clearly outlined?
- [ ] Are the implications clearly stated?

### Methodology
- [ ] Does the paper describe all the procedures done to obtain the results presented?
- [ ] Are sufficient details and or references provided?
- [ ] Are protocols logically grounded and organized into flow charts where applicable?
- [ ] Is there transition between different methods?
- [ ] Did you ensure that you did not state results in the methods section?
- [ ] Is the purpose stated clearly for any procedure whose function was not clear?
- [ ] Have ethical guidelines been carried out where appropriate?
- [ ] If you are training neural networks, random forests, or anything that is not linear regression, do you include a paragraph detailing the training design? E.g., hyperparameters such as the learning rate, optimizers used, loss, actual architecture, regularization used, final training and test loss, etc. This can certainly appear in the appendix but it has to appear somewhere.

### Results
- [ ] Did you report all the main findings as well as other important findings?
- [ ] Are your most important results and their interpretation provided at the beginning of the results section?
- [ ] Is the organization from most to least important within the paragraphs?
- [ ] Does each results paragraph or section have not only the data but also the interpretation?
- [ ] If Figures and Tables are used, are they alluded to in the text?
- [ ] Are Figure captions below the images and table captions above?
- [ ] Are control or baseline results included (if applicable)?

### Discussion, Conclusions, and Future Work
- [ ] Did you interpret key findings/ provide the answer to your research question?
- [ ] Is the answer stated precisely?
- [ ] Is the answer followed by supporting evidence?
- [ ] Is there a summary paragraph at the end of the discussion?
- [ ] Is the significance of the work highlighted?
- [ ] Did you compare/contrast your work with published work if possible?
- [ ] Did you explain any discrepancies or unexpected findings?
- [ ] Did you provide generalizations where possible?
- [ ] Are limitations of your work adequately addressed?
- [ ] Is future work discussed in depth?
